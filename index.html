Module 01 — Theory

Double & Triple Integration

A double integral extends integration to functions of two variables over a 2-D region. When the integrand is 1, it yields the area of the region. A triple integral extends this to 3-D, giving volume when the integrand is 1.

Area via Double Integral

For a Type I region where a ≤ x ≤ b and g₁(x) ≤ y ≤ g₂(x):

A = ∬R dA = ∫ab ∫g₁(x)g₂(x) dy dx
Inner integral first → result is a function of x → outer integral gives A

Worked Example — Parabola & Line

Find area bounded by y = x² and y = 2x.

Intersections: x² = 2x → x = 0 and x = 2.

A = ∫02 ∫x²2x dy dx = ∫02 (2x − x²) dx
= [ x² − x³/3 ]02 = 4 − 8/3
= 4/3 ≈ 1.3333

Volume via Triple Integral

For a region where a≤x≤b, g₁≤y≤g₂, h₁≤z≤h₂:

V = ∭E dV = ∫ab ∫g₁(x)g₂(x) ∫h₁(x,y)h₂(x,y) dz dy dx
Evaluate from innermost (z) outward

Worked Example — Unit Cube

V = ∫01 ∫01 ∫01 dz dy dx = 1
Volume = 1

Worked Example — Pyramid

Bounded by z = 0, z = 1−x−y, x≥0, y≥0, x+y≤1:

V = ∫01 ∫01−x ∫01−x−y dz dy dx
= ∫01 ∫01−x (1−x−y) dy dx = ∫01 (1−x)²/2 dx
= 1/6 ≈ 0.16667

Algorithm — Area (Double)
1
Identify the bounding curves of region R.
2
Solve for intersection points to get x limits [a, b].
3
Express y limits as g₁(x) and g₂(x).
4
Evaluate inner integral: ∫g₁g₂ dy = g₂−g₁.
5
Evaluate outer integral over [a,b] to get Area.
Algorithm — Volume (Triple)
1
Identify the 3-D solid region E.
2
Determine x limits [a, b] from the projection.
3
Express y limits g₁(x), g₂(x) for each x.
4
Express z limits h₁(x,y), h₂(x,y) for each (x,y).
5
Integrate innermost (z) → middle (y) → outer (x).

Problem: Find area bounded by y = x² and y = 2x.
1
Find intersections:x² = 2x → x(x−2) = 0
∴ x = 0, x = 2
2
Upper bound: At x=1: y=2x=2 > y=x²=1, so y=2x is top.
3
Set up:A = ∫₀² ∫x²2x dy dx
4
Inner integral:[y]x²2x = 2x − x²
5
Outer integral:∫₀² (2x−x²) dx = [x²−x³/3]₀²
= 4−8/3 = 4/3
∴ Area = 4/3 ≈ 1.3333 sq units

Intersections:x² = √x → x⁴ = x → x = 0, 1

3
Set up:A = ∫₀¹ ∫x²√x dy dx
4
Inner:√x − x²
5
Outer:∫₀¹ (√x−x²) dx = ⅔−⅓ = 1/3
∴ Area = 1/3 ≈ 0.3333 sq units

∭ Solved Examples
Problem: Find the volume of a unit cube where x, y, and z each go from 0 to 1.
1
Understand the solid: It's just a 1×1×1 cube. Each variable runs from 0 to 1 independently.x ∈ [0, 1], y ∈ [0, 1], z ∈ [0, 1]
2
Write the triple integral:V = ∫₀¹ ∫₀¹ ∫₀¹ dz dy dx
3
Step 1 — innermost integral (z):∫₀¹ dz = [z]₀¹ = 1 − 0 = 1
4
Step 2 — middle integral (y):∫₀¹ 1 dy = [y]₀¹ = 1
5
Step 3 — outer integral (x):∫₀¹ 1 dx = [x]₀¹ = 1
✓ Makes sense: 1×1×1 cube has volume 1
∴ Volume = 1 cubic unit
Problem: Find the volume of a box with dimensions 2 × 3 × 4 (x from 0→2, y from 0→3, z from 0→4).
1
Understand the solid: A simple rectangular box. All limits are constants — easy!x ∈ [0, 2], y ∈ [0, 3], z ∈ [0, 4]
2
Write the triple integral:V = ∫₀² ∫₀³ ∫₀⁴ dz dy dx
3
Inner (z): height of box∫₀⁴ dz = 4
4
Middle (y): width of box∫₀³ 4 dy = 4 × 3 = 12
5
Outer (x): depth of box∫₀² 12 dx = 12 × 2 = 24
✓ Same as length × width × height = 2×3×4 = 24
∴ Volume = 24 cubic units
Problem: Find the volume of a pyramid-like solid under z = 1−x−y, where x ≥ 0, y ≥ 0, and x+y ≤ 1. (z goes from 0 up to the sloped surface.)
1
Understand the solid: The base is a right triangle in the xy-plane. Above each point (x,y) the solid rises from z=0 to z=1−x−y.x ∈ [0,1], y ∈ [0, 1−x], z ∈ [0, 1−x−y]
2
Write the triple integral:V = ∫₀¹ ∫₀^(1−x) ∫₀^(1−x−y) dz dy dx
3
Inner (z) — integrate from 0 to 1−x−y:∫₀^(1−x−y) dz = (1 − x − y)
4
Middle (y) — integrate (1−x−y) from 0 to 1−x:∫₀^(1−x)(1−x−y) dy
= (1−x)y − y²/2 |₀^(1−x)
= (1−x)² − (1−x)²/2 = (1−x)²/2
5
Outer (x) — integrate (1−x)²/2 from 0 to 1:∫₀¹ (1−x)²/2 dx = [−(1−x)³/6]₀¹ = 1/6
∴ Volume = 1/6 ≈ 0.1667 cubic units
